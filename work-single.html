Navigating Disruptions in the Automobile Dealership Landscape

Overview: The automobile manufacturing and dealership industry, established in the 1920s, faces unprecedented challenges due to disruptive forces such as direct selling models by manufacturers (e.g., Tesla), partnerships with online retailers (e.g., Amazon), regulatory changes, and societal shifts towards electric vehicle adoption. To ensure the industry’s survivability, it is imperative to implement changes to the traditional dealership operation structures, which have remained consistent over the past century.

Key Disruptions:

  • Direct Selling Models: Manufacturers like Tesla are bypassing traditional dealerships, selling directly to consumers.
  • Online Retail Partnerships: Collaborations with online giants such as Amazon allow for vehicle purchases and home delivery, challenging the traditional dealership model.
  • Regulatory Actions: Increasing regulations aimed at reducing emissions and promoting electric vehicles.
  • Shift Towards Electric Vehicles: Growing societal and environmental demand for electric vehicles.

Research Methodology: In collaboration with Lace, the Eller Business Consulting Team conducted extensive research to define a desired future state (DFS) for automotive franchise dealerships. The research included:

  • 400+ hours of research
  • 15 live interviews with Royal Automotive Group employees

Opportunities and Challenges: A comprehensive SWOT analysis was conducted to identify opportunities and challenges for future growth. The analysis and subsequent recommendations were approached from four departmental perspectives:

  • Human Resources (HR): Focus on promoting customer centricity, new recruitment strategies, work flexibility, and AI usage.
  • Technology: Implementation of a robust customer-relationship-management system (CRM) and cybersecurity measures.
  • Finance and Operations: Introduction of flexible vehicle subscription plans and hospitality-inspired loyalty programs for operational efficiency.
  • Marketing: Development of creative market segmentation, brand positioning, and promotional strategies.

Pathway Transition Options: Two pathways were identified for dealerships to transition from the current state to the desired future state:

  • Conservative Pathway: A gradual approach to change, focusing on incremental improvements and risk mitigation.
  • Transformative Pathway: A bold approach, embracing significant changes and innovation to stay ahead of industry disruptions.

Each pathway includes a three-year implementation plan customized to the unique needs and capabilities of the dealership through an à la carte selection model.

Evaluation of Pathways: Each pathway was analyzed based on:

  • Customer Value
  • Investment Level
  • Rewards
  • Threats

Critical threats and potential rewards were evaluated, considering:

  • Macro changes in the industry
  • Competitive response
  • Market share
  • Customer loyalty
  • Cost per transaction

Strategic Recommendations: This report serves as an extension of the 2023 Modern Retail Organization final report and provides strategic recommendations for franchise dealerships to navigate industry disruptions. By understanding consumer preferences, retention strategies, value propositions, and how to effectively showcase these values, franchise dealerships can adapt to the evolving automotive landscape and thrive in the future.
